## Active Mistakes to Target Throughout This Session

Actively monitor and correct the learner whenever they make these errors:

1. **Verb form slips** — e.g. writing "comes with" instead of "come with" in a plural/base-form context.
2. **Subject-verb agreement** — e.g. "it saves" when the subject requires a different form.
3. **Word-form slips mid-sentence** — e.g. capitalising "Nowadays" when it appears mid-sentence.
4. **Unnatural collocations** — flag any collocation that a native speaker would not use; offer the natural alternative.
5. **Comparative modifier accuracy** — e.g. misusing "more" vs. "-er" forms or omitting "than".
6. **Mid-sentence tense switches** — flag any shift between present and past within a single idea.
7. **Countable/uncountable noun alignment** — e.g. treating "exercise" as countable in general statements.
8. **Incorrect preposition in collocations** — e.g. "crucial in" → should be "crucial for".

For each error caught, briefly explain the rule and ask the learner to self-correct before moving on.

---

## Session Sequence (30 minutes total)

### 1. Grammar — Countable and Uncountable Nouns (6 min)

Present 8 sentences on health and lifestyle topics. Each sentence has a gap where the learner must choose the correct quantifier or article (e.g. *much / many / a little / a few / some / any / Ø*) and justify whether the noun is countable or uncountable.

Include nouns: *exercise, advice, information, habit, progress, equipment, symptom, research*.

After the learner answers all 8, give a clear correction summary.

---

### 2. Reading — Health Advice Article (7 min)

Provide a short article (120–150 words) at B2 level giving health advice related to diet, sleep, and exercise.

Then ask the learner:
- One gist question (main message of the article).
- Two detail questions (specific facts from the article).
- One vocabulary-in-context question (ask what a word/phrase means as used in the text).

---

### 3. Vocabulary — Health Theme (5 min)

Give the learner 6 health-related collocations with one word missing (e.g. *___ a balanced diet*, *___ regular exercise*, *boost the immune ___*).

After answers, ask them to write two original sentences using collocations they found difficult.

---

### 4. Writing — Advice with Natural Collocations (7 min)

Task: Write a short paragraph (80–100 words) giving advice to a friend who wants to improve their health. Use at least 4 natural health collocations and at least 2 modal verbs for advice (*should, ought to, had better, it is advisable to*).

Evaluate for: collocation accuracy, grammar (especially modal + bare infinitive, countable/uncountable), cohesion, and vocabulary range.

---

### 5. Speaking — Healthy Habit (5 min)

Prompt: *"Describe a healthy habit you have or would like to develop. Explain why it is important and how it benefits your daily life."*

Ask the learner to respond in 4–6 sentences. Evaluate for: fluency, collocation use, grammar accuracy (target the active mistake list above), and vocabulary.

---

### 6. Optional Listening (if time allows)

Describe a short scenario: a doctor is giving a patient advice about lifestyle changes. Ask the learner to predict 3 pieces of advice the doctor might give and write them as reported speech (e.g. *The doctor said that he should…*).
